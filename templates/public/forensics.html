EMPLOYMENT LAW

The "Constructive Dismissal" Deep Dive

The Scenario: An employee resigns after their role is fundamentally changed without consent.

The Deep Dive: We don't just read the resignation letter. We trace the history of contract variations and management emails. We cross-reference this timeline specifically against Section 95(1)(c) of the Employment Rights Act 1996 to prove the employer's conduct breached the "trust and confidence" term.

> REF: Employment Rights Act 1996 (s.95)

> EVIDENCE: Email_Archive_May24.zip

> FINDING: Unilateral change to hours (Date: 12/05)

> LEGAL TEST: "Fundamental Breach" threshold met.

> CONCLUSION: Valid claim for Constructive Dismissal.

HOUSING COMPLIANCE

The "Section 21" Invalidity Check

The Scenario: A landlord attempts a "no-fault" eviction.

The Deep Dive: We audit the entire tenancy lifecycle. We check the deposit protection dates, the EPC rating history, and the Gas Safety Record timestamps. By applying Section 33 of the Deregulation Act 2015, we can prove the eviction notice is legally void if a single document was served late.

> REF: Deregulation Act 2015 (s.33)

> EVIDENCE: Tenancy_Pack_Signed.pdf

> AUDIT: Gas Cert dated 4 days post-occupation.

> LEGAL TEST: Prescribed Requirements NOT met.

> CONCLUSION: Section 21 Notice is VOID.

COMMERCIAL / CONSUMER

The "Reject Goods" Timeline

The Scenario: A high-value asset (e.g., Vehicle) fails after 5 months.

The Deep Dive: We analyze the diagnostic logs and repair history. We apply the "Reverse Burden of Proof" found in Section 19(14) of the Consumer Rights Act 2015. Our report mathematically proves the fault was inherent at the point of sale, shifting the legal liability back to the dealer.

> REF: Consumer Rights Act 2015 (s.19-14)

> EVIDENCE: Diagnostic_Log_v2.csv

> TIMELINE: Fault occurred at Month 5 (Under 6mo).

> LEGAL TEST: Burden of proof lies with Seller.

> CONCLUSION: Full Right to Reject applies.
